MONIKA BURKE

monikaburke@epicart.com

As a child Monika could be seen traipsing through the wooded trails of her home city of Brentwood, Tennessee. Monika was never far from nature or the little critters that lived in it, and often brought these animals home with her to keep as pets. However, from mudpies and bugs to the prestige halls of Yale, Monika evolved from her tomboyish childhood into an exemplary student of the Arts who excelled in all her courses. She was an active member in her Sorority and received her Master's degree at the age of twenty-six. In her spare time Monika likes to play her harp in her herb garden alongside her cat, Jasper. She also enjoys cooking and often hosts dinner parties for her friends and colleagues accompanied by musical pieces she's written and artistically crafted hors d'oevres. GUSTAV FREDRICKSON

gustavfredrickson@epicart.com

Gustav was born and raised in the bustling city of Munich, Germany. From a young age, Gustav showed a vivid interest in the arts, and excelled in all his studies. When he was fourteen, he and his parents immigrated to America and settled in Fayetteville, West Virginia. Despite having to overcome the challenges of transferring to a new school with only a mild grasp on English, Gustav proved his proficiency and willingness to persevere and overcome. By the age of eighteen he had been offered a scholarship at the Rhode Island School of Design where he studied until he received his Master's Degree in Fine Arts. From there Gustav officially began his career as an artist and made a name for himself amongst the greats, earning his art a place in several museums. Furthermore, Gustav is an active member of and partner of WWF, husband to Catherine and father to Hannah and Lionel, as well as an avid fisher, vlogger, and environmental activist.

THERESA JACKSON

theresajackson@epicart.com

Theresa, age 36, grew up in Houston, Texas with her parents, four siblings, and grandmother. Now having four kids of her own, one of which has special needs, she understands the creativity and imagination of a child as well as the need for patience better than anyone, making her the most qualified to handle our Art for Tots class. When she isn't pursuing her passion in teaching you can find this southern woman bustling about the kitchen concocting a new recipe, over one of her favorite romantic novels, or clipping coupons to fuel her couponing hobby. She also shares a passion for cross-stitching, sewing, and knitting which she can sometimes be seen working on during lunch breaks.

EDDIE RIVAS

edmundrivas@epicart.com

Edmund, or Eddie as he's more commonly known to those close to him, is a man who sees the world through rose colored glasses; his relaxed and abstract approach to life comes from his abuelito. Eddie was raised by his grandfather from the age of four when his mother passed away from lung cancer in his hometown of Stockton, California. Though times were hard, Eddie's grandfather encouraged and taught him to see the good side of everything rather than the bad, but at seventeen years old Eddie's grandfather passed away. From that point in his life he made the decision to change his career path and start something that would inspire people. He graduated high school, moved to Fresno, California, and there he began putting his emotions onto canvas in honor of his grandfather. Eddie sold his creations on the streets of Fresno for years until his name became popular in the area and he made enough money to put himself through art school. Now twenty-seven, Eddie teaches at EPIC Art Studio with a bachelor's degree and a desire to spread his grandfather's good will and kind spirit to his students.

BEN ROMERO

benjaminromero@epicart.com

Even as a kid Ben has always put his family first, and art was a gateway for him to show his love and appreciation to those he cared about. Whether it was macaroni sculptures, crayola masterpieces, or dying his poodle with kool-aid, Ben always found a way to bring color and imagination to his and everyone else's world. He led a relatively normal life alongside his sister, Pamela, who never failed to find trouble for the two of them when they were together. As he got older, Ben found himself fixing vehicles alongside his father who owned a local automotive repair shop, and there he spent most of his teenage years learning how cars worked and what it took to repair them, but he never had a passion for it. It was only when Ben entered his thirties and met his down-to-earth wife, Samantha that he truly realized what he wanted to do. Now close to a decade later of hard work and schooling, Ben once again spreads his love of art to people.
